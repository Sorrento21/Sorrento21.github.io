    <h1 style="font-size: 28px; color: #2C3E50; text-align: center;">Smart Artistic Vase: Interactive Plant Care</h1>

    <!-- Project Description -->
    <p style="font-size: 18px; line-height: 1.6; color: #34495E; text-align: justify;">
        This project focuses on the concept of interactive design, aiming to give plants the ability to express their needs like pets. The goal is to help plant enthusiasts take better care of their plants while guiding beginners on how to care for them properly. The smart vase serves as a communication tool between plants and their caretakers, making the process more engaging and intuitive.
    </p>

    <!-- Image Section -->
    <div style="text-align: center; margin: 20px 0;">
        <img src="/images/vase.png" alt="Smart Vase Image" style="max-width: 100%; border: 2px solid #ccc; border-radius: 5px;">
    </div>

    <p style="font-size: 18px; line-height: 1.6; color: #34495E; text-align: justify;">
        The vase works on the principle of bioelectricity, where plants generate electrical signals in response to changes in their environment or growth conditions. The vase detects these signals using electrodes to determine the plant's status, including its need for water, nutrients, or other care.
    </p>

    <!-- Interactive Logic Description -->
    <h2 style="font-size: 24px; color: #2980B9;">Interactive Logic</h2>
    <p style="font-size: 18px; line-height: 1.6; color: #34495E; text-align: justify;">
        1. When a user approaches, if the plant has physiological needs, it generates bioelectric signals that trigger the vase to emit baby-like cries, notifying the user to take care of the plant. Different needs are indicated by different sounds, allowing the user to easily understand what the plant requires.<br>
        2. The vase continuously senses the environmental conditions, and the patterns on the vase gradually change to guide users on when to water or fertilize the plant. If the action is performed too quickly, the pattern will not be obvious; if the action is delayed too long, the pattern will fade.
    </p>
