Student Dormitory Wayfinding System Design

Project Overview

After all students in the major went on a study trip to Europe, we formed groups to design a wayfinding system for student dormitories. We aimed to create a system that harmonizes with the style of student dormitories, is distinctive, and exudes elegance and aesthetics. Through investigation and analysis of the dormitories, we chose to use lines to connect the entire wayfinding system, achieving a coherent, aesthetically pleasing, and innovative unity.

Preliminary Research

Our student dormitories are located in the Xinya College Apartments. The interior walls are minimally decorated, mostly consisting of black and white straight lines, with many blank wall spaces. We decided to utilize mural paintings to make use of these blank wall spaces. Considering the diverse styles of interior decoration and the potential visual clutter, we avoided overly complex or stylized wayfinding systems. Lastly, the spirit of Xinya College embodies innovation and elegance. Taking all these factors into account, we chose a design centered around lines for the entire wayfinding system.

Design Outcome

We first created an AutoCAD model of the entire dormitory building, labeling all locations that required directional signs. In terms of sign design, we aimed to use coherent lines to outline a figure’s actions, conveying clear and precise meanings.

In the first-floor lobby, we specially designed a comprehensive guide that clearly indicates all facilities on each floor, providing students with an overall understanding of each floor’s functions.

It’s worth mentioning that all our designs are based on the actual situation of the dormitory, ensuring practical application. We considered the specific placement of signs on walls, the optimal placement locations, and the real needs of students. We also created realistic proportioned renderings to showcase the final presentation effect.
